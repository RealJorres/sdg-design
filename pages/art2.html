iUgpong: Connecting Internet Underprivileged Villages of Marinduque



Photo information here


January, 2023 - In the Philippines, access to reliable internet remains a critical issue for remote communities, particularly in geographically isolated areas like Marinduque. For many, high-speed connectivity is beyond reach due to rugged terrains and lack of infrastructure, leading to barriers in education, communication, economic development, and social engagement. In response to these challenges, an innovative program spearheaded by the College of Information and Computing Sciences (CICS) is pioneering the use of satellite technology to connect Marinduque’s remote villages with affordable, high-speed internet.

The CICS-led initiative aims to bridge Marinduque’s digital divide by introducing a Satellite Internet system combined with a Wi-Fi network designed specifically to cater to isolated communities. Satellite technology, unlike conventional mobile networks, does not rely on ground-based infrastructure, allowing it to deliver consistent, high-speed internet in areas where connectivity would otherwise be impossible. This is a significant advantage for regions like Marinduque, where mountainous terrains and island locations make traditional internet delivery methods impractical.

Mongpong, a small islet in the municipality of Santa Cruz with over 1,000 residents, serves as a model village for the project. Here, the new internet system is already changing lives, with more than 300 individuals now able to access online resources for education, economic growth, and personal development. By enabling digital engagement in these underserved areas, the initiative opens up new avenues for learning, communication, and economic opportunity.

The project supports three key Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs): Quality Education (SDG 4), Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure (SDG 9), and Sustainable Cities and Communities (SDG 11). By offering high-speed internet access, it enables students to participate in online learning, educators to access up-to-date resources, and local businesses to leverage digital platforms. The generated income also sustains technology costs and funds free internet access for DepEd schools and barangays, supporting local educational institutions and empowering communities.

The project’s phased approach ensures each step is effectively implemented to maximize connectivity outcomes.

  1. Planning: Initial planning involved assessing the connectivity needs of remote villages and determining the technical requirements.
  2. Network Design: Expert network designers crafted a Wi-Fi system suited to Marinduque's unique terrain, ensuring strong, reliable signals reach even the most isolated areas.
  3. Installation and Testing: The satellite technology and Wi-Fi infrastructure were deployed and tested in real-world conditions, with adjustments made as necessary.
  4. Ongoing Monitoring and Support: Dedicated network administrators and support technicians continue to monitor the system, ensuring consistent performance and troubleshooting any issues that arise.

The project requires a significant yet manageable financial investment. An initial cost of PHP 100,000 covered installation and setup, while PHP 40,000 is allocated for yearly operational expenses. Key resources include human expertise—network designers, installers, administrators, and support technicians—and technology resources, such as satellite systems, Wi-Fi vending machines, fiber optics kits, and other essential networking devices. By generating revenue, the project sustains its operational expenses, supporting long-term connectivity and growth.

Traditional methods of connectivity, such as mobile networks, are either non-existent or unreliable in these regions. This satellite-based initiative offers a powerful alternative, allowing internet coverage to extend across mountainous areas and water-bound villages without dependency on physical infrastructure like cell towers or cables.

The project demonstrates how satellite technology can transform internet access in remote communities, setting a replicable model for rural digital connectivity across other regions of the Philippines. By connecting Marinduque’s isolated villages, the CICS-led program not only enhances individual and community opportunities but also drives forward the country’s digital inclusivity, underscoring the importance of high-speed, affordable internet for all.
